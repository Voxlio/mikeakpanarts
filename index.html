The Artist Biography

Universal Master

"After God's creation, the remaining part was left for an artist."

Akparawa Mike Akpan is a Nigerian artist whose medium and style cut across cultures. He has successfully freed himself from ethnic constraints that often stifle creative expression, producing outstanding works with truly universal themes. Drawing inspiration from the richness of his Ibibio culture, he displays a complete mastery and control of his chosen medium: oil on canvas.

Born in 1970 into the family of Nung Emem in Ndiya, Nsit Ubium L.G.A., Akwa Ibom State, Mike's imagination was tickled from childhood by traditional folk tales. He grew up among established traditional wood carvers, sculptors, and weavers of colorful raffia mats, inheriting a legacy of intricate forms and vivid colors.

Mastering the abstract oil painting style, Mike's work is celebrated for its richness, freshness, and clarity. His process is one of visionary motivation; each visual feast of colors he presents leaves his audience completely overwhelmed. Intellectually inclined admirers often find themselves stimulated into "mental gymnastics" trying to decode Mike's intricately woven forms.

"His work invites both immediate emotional response and deeper reflection, offering viewers a rich visual experience shaped by inspiration and cultural memory."
